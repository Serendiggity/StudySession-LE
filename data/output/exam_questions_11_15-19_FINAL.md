## ✅ Q15: PURPOSE of Newspaper Publication - ANSWERED

**Question:** What is the purpose of publishing the Notice of Bankruptcy and First Meeting of Creditors in a local newspaper?

**Answer:** ✅ **(b) To inform the general public with whom the debtor is dealing that he is no longer operating**

**Direct Quote:**
> "At least five days before the meeting (in an ordinary bankruptcy), the Trustee must publish a notice in the local newspaper announcing details of the meeting. This is for the benefit of any other creditors who may have been omitted from the list in the Statement of Affairs. **The notice also explains the bankrupt's legal status to anyone who may do business with him.**"

**Source:** Insolvency Administration Course Material, Section 10.3.8 (page 148)

**Cross-Reference:** BIA §102(4) (publication requirement), §102(5) (meeting purpose)

**Rationale:** The quote explicitly states the notice "explains the bankrupt's legal status to anyone who may do business with him" - this matches option (b) about informing the general public with whom the debtor is dealing. Option (a) is wrong (not about asset sales), (c) is wrong (not about future dealings), (d) is wrong (not about advertising).

## ✅ Q17: DEFINITION of Newspaper - ANSWERED

**Question:** Which one of the following is included in the definition of a newspaper?

**Answer:** ✅ **(b) A weekly newspaper published locally**

**Definition (BC Interpretation Act §29):**
> "newspaper means a printed publication in sheet form, intended for general circulation, **published regularly at intervals of not longer than a week**, consisting in great part of news of current events of general interest"

**Source:** British Columbia Interpretation Act (applicable to Canadian bankruptcy law interpretation)

**Key Criteria:**
- ✅ Published **at intervals not longer than a week** = weekly or more frequent
- ✅ General circulation
- ✅ News of current events

**Why each option:**
- **(a) Magazine** - ❌ Usually monthly/quarterly (longer than a week)
- **(b) Weekly newspaper published locally** - ✅ Meets "not longer than a week" + "general circulation" + "local" matches BIA §102(4) "local newspaper"
- **(c) Specialized publication** - ❌ Not "general circulation"
- **(d) Weekend edition of a newspaper** - ❌ Not a separate "newspaper" (it's part of a newspaper, not published regularly on its own)

**Note:** Federal Interpretation Act does NOT define "newspaper" - provincial interpretation acts apply.

## ⚠️ Q11: Partnership Assignment Execution - PROBABLE (No Direct Quote)

**Question:** If all the members of an unlimited partnership decide to file an assignment in bankruptcy, who will execute the assignment documents?

**Probable Answer:** **(d) All the partners are required to sign all the assignment documents**

**Evidence Found:**

**BIA Rules §114 - Bankrupt Partnerships:**
> "A partnership that is bankrupt shall submit to the trustee a statement of its partnership affairs, verified by one of the partners or by the manager in charge of the partnership affairs, and **each bankrupt partner shall submit** a statement of their own personal affairs."

**Directive 16R §7:**
> "Where a partnership is involved, a Statement of Affairs must be prepared for the business **as well as for each of the partners making an assignment**."

**BIA §85(1):**
> "on **all the general partners** of a limited partnership becoming bankrupt, the property of the limited partnership vests in the trustee."

**Analysis:**
- §114: "**each bankrupt partner**" (plural) must submit personal statements
- Directive 16R: "**each of the partners making an assignment**" (plural)
- §85: "**all the general partners**" (plural)

**Inference:** The consistent use of plural language ("each", "all") suggests all partners must participate in the assignment process.

**LIMITATION:** ⚠️ **NO DIRECT QUOTE** found stating "all partners must sign the assignment documents." The sources address:
- Statements of affairs (§114)
- Property vesting (§85)
- NOT assignment document execution

**Status:** PROBABLE based on inference, but not definitively quoted.
